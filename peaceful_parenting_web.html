Peaceful Parenting Questionnaire

Why This Enhanced Format Works Better:

Research shows that when we're faced with completely open-ended questions, our minds can become overwhelmed by too much freedom. This paradox of choice creates stress and often leads to mental blocks. By providing guiding examples and inspirational reflections, we create a supportive framework that reduces anxiety and encourages deeper, more authentic responses. Think of it as offering stepping stones across a river - the destination is the same, but the journey becomes much more accessible.

Use these questions to enhance your learning and set your intention. Research shows that thinking and writing about our emotions helps with healing, growth, and emotional integration.
1
What would you say is your current level of "well-being" on a scale from 1 to 10?

Guiding Examples:

  • 3-4: I feel overwhelmed most of the time, constantly reactive
  • 5-6: I have good days and bad days, sometimes I handle situations well
  • 7-8: I generally feel balanced, with manageable moments of stress
  • 9-10: I feel at peace and confident in my role as a parent

Well-being is not the absence of challenges, but the presence of tools to navigate with grace.
2
What mantras or "antidotes" might be helpful to you when you get upset?

Guiding Examples:

  • "This too shall pass"
  • "I am the adult here, I can stay calm"
  • "My child is learning, not being difficult"
  • "I breathe deeply and choose love"
  • "This moment doesn't define our day"
Practice doesn't make perfect, it makes progress. And progress is enough.

Remember: This is a growth process, not a perfection goal. Every small step toward peaceful parenting is a victory.

The words we speak to ourselves become the world in which we live.
3
If your child had to pick three words to describe you, what would he or she say?

Guiding Examples:

  • Positive aspects: loving, fun, patient, understanding
  • Areas to improve: stressed, busy, loud, impatient
Our children are the most honest mirror of who we are when no one else is watching.
4
If your child had to pick three words to describe his or her relationship with you, what would he or she say?

Guiding Examples:

  • Positive relationship: safe, fun, close, trustworthy
  • Relationship to improve: tense, distant, unpredictable, conflicted
The relationship we build today with our children determines the trust they'll have in us tomorrow.
5
What would you like your child to feel about him or herself, coming out of growing up with you?

Guiding Examples:

  • "I am valuable just as I am"
  • "I can trust my parents no matter what"
  • "It's safe to make mistakes and learn"
  • "I am capable of facing challenges"
  • "I deserve unconditional love"
Children don't need perfect parents, they need parents who love them unconditionally.
6
Who would you like your child to become as a result of growing up with you?

Guiding Examples:

  • A confident and empathetic person
  • Someone who knows how to regulate their emotions
  • A person who contributes positively to the world
  • Someone who maintains close family connections
  • A resilient and authentic person
We don't raise children to be perfect, we raise them to be fully human.
7
What are your strengths as a parent?

Guiding Examples:

  • I am loving and expressive
  • I actively listen to my child
  • I'm consistent with routines
  • I have a good sense of humor
  • I'm protective without being overprotective
Recognizing our strengths is not vanity, it's the foundation for building from a place of self-love.
8
What do you want more of in your relationship with your child or children?

Guiding Examples:

  • More authentic emotional connection
  • More moments of fun and laughter
  • More patience in difficult moments
  • More open and honest communication
  • More presence instead of distractions
Connection isn't built in big moments, but in small daily moments.
9
What might you be willing to do to make that relationship happen?

Guiding Examples:

  • Dedicate 15 minutes daily of exclusive time
  • Practice breathing before reacting
  • Ask for forgiveness when I make mistakes
  • Seek professional support if needed
  • Create routines that foster connection
Real change begins with small consistent actions, not with occasional grand gestures.
10
Set your intention for this course. Describe your highest and best vision for yourself as a parent.

Begin with three words that describe the parent you're striving to be:

  • Patient, present, loving
  • Balanced, understanding, firm
  • Calm, connected, authentic
Parenting is not about creating perfect children, but about becoming the best version of ourselves.

PRACTICE: STOP, DROP & BREATHE

Sometime in the next week, your child will make you mad. When this happens, practice:

STOP

Just stop. Close your mouth.

DROP

Drop your agenda.

BREATHE

Take a few deep breaths.

Use a mantra to change your perspective

Move your body to release the emotion

CHOOSE LOVE - Make the conscious choice to calm down
